# Campaign History & snapshots

Operational guidance for Game Masters using **Campaign History** and snapshot comparison.

Snapshots are **historical captures of projected campaign state** — factions, NPC presence, rumors, danger, and location context at a moment in chronology. They are derived from wiki and chronology data. They are not save files, alternate timelines, or player-facing fiction on their own.

---

## Kinds of snapshots

| Kind | How it is created |
|------|-------------------|
| **Visit** | Automatic when you mark a location as visited by the party |
| **Milestone** | Manual checkpoint from Campaign History |
| **Manual** | Explicit labeled capture (milestone family) |

**Archived** snapshots are retained for history but may not be available for comparison. Only **hot** snapshots with full payload data can be compared.

---

## When to capture a milestone

- End of a story arc
- Before or after a time skip
- Start or end of a war or major conflict
- After a major discovery or revelation
- When faction power shifts significantly

Visit snapshots happen automatically when you mark locations visited — you do not need to duplicate those unless you also want a named milestone.

---

## What comparison shows

Comparing an **earlier moment** to a **later moment** summarizes regional change:

- Faction and organization stance shifts
- NPC presence and movement
- Rumors and party knowledge deltas
- Danger level changes
- Location-related evolution in the projection

Comparison always runs **older → newer**. The UI swaps reversed selections automatically.

**DM vs party view:** DMs see the full operational diff. Player-facing surfaces use party projection only.

---

## Limits (v1)

- **Two saved moments required** — comparing to live world state is not available in v1.
- **Region anchor required** — campaign-wide milestones without a location anchor cannot be compared region-to-region yet.
- **Archived snapshots** cannot be compared.

---

## Where to find it

Open **Chronology → Feed** (DM or Co-DM). Scroll to **Campaign History** at the bottom of the feed view.

Use **What are snapshots?** in the app for a quick summary.
